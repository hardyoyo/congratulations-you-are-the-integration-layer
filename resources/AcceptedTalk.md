# Congratulations, You’re the Integration Layer Now: OpenTelemetry in the UC Stack 

## abstract
OpenTelemetry is not new. Many of us have heard of it. Some of us have experimented with it. What has changed is the vendors. Observability platforms increasingly ship OpenTelemetry support as a first-class integration model. Gartner describes it as early mainstream and widely supported across vendors, and a recent European interoperability assessment highlights adoption across more than 40 providers. It is quickly becoming the default path for telemetry. And that shift has consequences. When vendors standardize on OpenTelemetry, they assume someone owns the pipeline: instrumentation, collectors, routing, sampling, cost control, and governance. Increasingly, that someone is the platform team. Congratulations — you’re the integration layer now. Attendees will learn how to recognize this industry shift and adapt their observability strategy. The session includes lessons learned from implementing an OpenTelemetry endpoint for GitHub Enterprise at UCSF, along with practical guidance that applies to other vendor integrations.
